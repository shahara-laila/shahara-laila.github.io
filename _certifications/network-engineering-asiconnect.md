## About the Program

The Asi@Connect Female Network Engineer Training Program is a regional initiative supporting women in network engineering and ICT across Asia. This intensive training equipped me with practical networking skills covering the full lifecycle of campus network design and administration.

## Key Topics Covered

- **Campus network design** — topology planning, equipment selection, structured cabling
- **Routing and switching** — static and dynamic routing (RIP, OSPF), switching fundamentals
- **IPv4 / IPv6** — addressing, subnetting, dual-stack configuration
- **VLANs** — virtual LAN configuration, inter-VLAN routing, trunking (802.1Q)
- **Network monitoring and management** — SNMP, network management tools, performance metrics
- **Network troubleshooting** — systematic fault isolation, common failure modes, diagnostic tools

## Why It Matters to My Work

Understanding network infrastructure is foundational for deploying AI systems in institutional settings — whether configuring servers for a university computer lab or deploying ML models in a clinical network. This training complements my software and research skills with systems-level knowledge.

## Key Takeaways

Participating in a women-focused engineering program reinforced my commitment to supporting diversity in STEM. The hands-on experience with real network equipment gave me practical infrastructure knowledge that informs how I think about deploying and maintaining production systems in academic and research environments.
